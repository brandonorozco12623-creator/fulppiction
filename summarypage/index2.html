Pulp Fiction

Movie Summary & Cast Information

Movie Summary

Plot Overview

Pulp Fiction is a 1994 neo-noir crime film that weaves together multiple interconnected storylines in a non-linear narrative. The film follows the lives of two mob hitmen, a boxer, a gangster's wife, and a pair of diner bandits in a series of tales of violence and redemption in Los Angeles.

Main Storylines

Vincent Vega & Jules Winnfield: Two philosophical hitmen retrieve a mysterious briefcase for their boss, crime lord Marsellus Wallace. Their journey leads them through various encounters, including a "miracle" that changes Jules' perspective on life.

The Gold Watch: Boxer Butch Coolidge is paid to throw a fight but instead wins, betraying Marsellus Wallace. He must retrieve his father's precious gold watch while evading Wallace's wrath.

The Bonnie Situation: Vincent accidentally kills a man in the car, leading to a frantic cleanup operation with the help of their friend Winston "The Wolf" Wolfe.

Vincent & Mia: Vincent takes Marsellus Wallace's wife Mia out for an evening, which takes a dangerous turn when Mia overdoses on heroin she mistakes for cocaine.

Themes

The film explores themes of redemption, fate, violence, and pop culture. Tarantino masterfully blends dark humor with intense violence, creating a unique cinematic experience that redefined independent filmmaking and influenced countless directors.

Main Cast

John Travolta as Vincent Vega

A smooth-talking hitman with a philosophical bent and a heroin addiction. Travolta's iconic performance revitalized his career.

Samuel L. Jackson as Jules Winnfield

Vincent's partner, a quotation-spouting hitman who experiences a spiritual awakening after a "miracle" during a job.

Bruce Willis as Butch Coolidge

An aging boxer who refuses to throw a fight as planned, leading to a dangerous game of cat and mouse with Marsellus Wallace.

Uma Thurman as Mia Wallace

The enigmatic wife of crime boss Marsellus Wallace, whose night out with Vincent takes a near-fatal turn.

Ving Rhames as Marsellus Wallace

A powerful crime boss whose authority is tested throughout the film's interconnected stories.

Harvey Keitel as Winston "The Wolf" Wolfe

A professional problem solver who helps clean up a bloody mess with efficiency and style.

Tim Roth as "Pumpkin" / Ringo

A small-time robber who, along with his girlfriend, decides to rob a diner in the film's opening and closing scenes.

Amanda Plummer as "Honey Bunny" / Yolanda

Pumpkin's volatile girlfriend and partner in crime during the diner robbery.
